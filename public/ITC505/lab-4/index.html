Leveraging Machine Learning for Early Prediction of Lifestyle Diseases: A Data-Driven Approach

Author: S. Harish

Published by: IEEE

Date: September 2024

Abstract

Machine learning techniques are increasingly being utilized to predict lifestyle diseases such as diabetes and hypertension early on. This paper presents a comprehensive data-driven approach to leverage machine learning algorithms for early diagnosis, focusing on real-world datasets and predictive model efficiency.

Introduction

With the rise in lifestyle diseases globally, there is a growing demand for innovative solutions to tackle this challenge. This paper investigates the potential of machine learning in healthcare, particularly for early diagnosis of lifestyle diseases.

Methodology

The methodology involves the collection of healthcare data from various sources, pre-processing techniques to clean and prepare the data, and the application of machine learning algorithms such as Decision Trees, Random Forests, and Neural Networks.

Results

The results from the study indicate a high degree of accuracy in predicting diseases such as diabetes and hypertension, with Neural Networks showing the highest predictive performance.

Conclusion

The paper concludes that machine learning is a promising tool for the early prediction of lifestyle diseases, offering the potential to significantly improve healthcare outcomes.

Stylistic Choices

Font Choices

For the main text, I chose Lora, a serif font, to create an academic feel that aligns with the formal nature of the essay. For the headers, I used Montserrat, a clean sans-serif font, to add contrast and improve readability.

Font Sizes

The h1 header is sized at 2.5em (up to 3em on larger screens) for immediate visual impact. h2 headers are sized at 1.8em to distinguish them from the main text while keeping them noticeable but not overpowering. The main body text is set to 1.1em to maintain readability without overwhelming the viewer with large chunks of text.

Color Choices

A soft blue (#4A90E2) was selected for the headers and link hover states to create a sense of calmness and professionalism. It contrasts well with the light background color. The background color for the entire page is a light gray (#f9f9f9), which is easy on the eyes, with white sections for the main article and header to create distinct content areas. The text color is a dark gray (#333333) for readability, contrasted with lighter gray (#555555) for footer text.

Margins and Padding

The layout uses generous padding around the header and article sections for a spacious and open feel. Margin adjustments for the headings, paragraphs, and list items help keep the layout consistent and visually balanced.

HTML Abuse Adjustments

The <h1> tag was used as the title's highest-level heading to establish proper document hierarchy, instead of previously using a lower-level heading tag solely for visual effect.
